Strengths & Weaknesses

Acquisition 1

Strengths:

  • Exceptional revenue generation (over 6.3M Robux monthly)
  • Strong day-1 retention (12.53%)
  • Double the revenue per user compared to Acquisition 2
  • Large and active user base
  • Diverse revenue streams including significant Pass revenue

Weaknesses:

  • Higher operational costs (20% LiveOps expenses)
  • DAU showing some decline over the observed period
  • Revenue volatility with significant peaks and valleys

Acquisition 2

Strengths:

  • Lower operational costs (17.5%)
  • More stable revenue pattern
  • Better traffic source diversification
  • Strong growth in user acquisition
  • More consistent DAU relative to benchmarks

Weaknesses:

  • Weaker day-1 retention (7.05%)
  • Lower revenue per user (half of Acquisition 1)
  • Lower overall revenue generation

Investment Decision Assessment

Both acquisitions represent extraordinarily successful investments:

  1. ROI Performance: Both games delivered ROIs over 23,000%, dramatically exceeding the 300% minimum requirement.
  2. Acquisition Value: Both games were purchased at prices far below their maximum value for a 300% ROI target.
  3. Relative Performance: Acquisition 1 shows superior financial performance and user engagement metrics, making it the stronger performer despite slightly higher operational costs.

Key Improvement Hypotheses

1. Lead Quality Enhancement

Focus on games with metrics similar to these successful acquisitions (strong retention and monetization). Implement a pre-qualification system that identifies games with >10% day-1 retention and >$0.50 revenue per DAU.

2. Response Rate Optimization

Personalize outreach with data-driven insights to improve the 55% response rate. Create templates highlighting successful case studies like Acquisitions 1 & 2 to demonstrate value to potential sellers.

3. Negotiation Effectiveness

Develop valuation models based on retention and monetization metrics to close more deals. Create tiered offer structures with performance-based incentives to align interests.

4. Acquisition Speed

Streamline due diligence to reduce friction in the final acquisition stage. Offer transitional support packages to address developer concerns and facilitate smoother transitions.

5. Portfolio Strategy

Balance between high-retention games (like Acquisition 1) and lower-cost operations (like Acquisition 2). Allocate budget specifically for games in emerging genres with high growth potential.

6. Post-Acquisition Integration

Standardize the operational improvements that made these acquisitions successful. Develop a cross-promotion strategy between acquired games to boost retention and monetization.

By implementing these changes, Indigo VC could potentially increase its lead-to-acquisition conversion from 12.3% to 20-25%, significantly expanding its portfolio with high-performing games.
